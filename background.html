Hypertension Management that markedly cuts the need for clinical input

Our trailblazing clinician-patient interface automates the management of your hypertensive patients.

By safely guiding patients in a stepwise management of their condition it significantly reduces the number of clinical contacts required. This results in time saved for both clinicians and patients.

Built to automatically follow NICE Guidelines.

Our innovative time saving app has been designed by clinicians for clinicians. It has been carefully constructed to follow evidenced based NICE Guidelines with safety inbuilt at every step. We have ensured that both our clinician and patient interfaces are simple to use. We are accredited by the MHRA as a Class I Medical Device. Four Simple Steps

  1. The clinician selects a tailored hypertension treatment plan on our Clinician Interface.
  2. The patient downloads Optimise BP on their smart phone.
  3. The App safely guides the patient through their bespoke hypertension treatment plan to lower their blood pressure.
  4. Optimise BP prompts safe titration of treatment until blood pressure reaches individualised treatment target.

The Team

Dr Fraser Booth

Dr Booth graduated from Guy’s, King’s and St Thomas Medical School in 2003. After working as an emergency medicine physician, he left the institutionalised world of medicine to become a medtech innovator. During the last 20 years he worked in Medical IT, seeking to develop solutions to improve the healthcare and wellbeing of individuals.

Dr Daniel Carlton-Conway

Following a foundation in Law and Business, Dr Carlton-Conway graduated from Kings College Medical School, London in 2003. He currently works in the NHS as a GP, an Allergy Specialist and has extensive board level management experience in the healthcare sector. Dr Carlton-Conway brings clinical and practical experience with a strong focus on patient safety.

Dr Brian Fisher MBE

Dr Fisher has 42 years of experience working as a General Practitioner in London. He has a particular interest in user involvement in the NHS and was at the forefront of work to enable patient access to their own medical records nationwide.
